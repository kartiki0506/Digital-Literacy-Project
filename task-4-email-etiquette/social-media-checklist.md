# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for responsible and professional social media use 
as a B.Tech student.

---

## ✅ DO'S

1. Keep your profiles professional — use a decent photo and appropriate bio.
2. Share your academic achievements, projects, and certifications.
3. Think before you post — ask yourself if you'd be comfortable 
   with your professor seeing it.
4. Use privacy settings to control who sees your personal posts.
5. Engage positively — like, comment, and share meaningful 
   and helpful content.

---

## ❌ DON'TS

1. Don't share personal information like phone number, address, 
   or ID details publicly.
2. Don't post offensive, political, or controversial content 
   that could harm your reputation.
3. Don't cyberbully or make negative comments about classmates, 
   teachers, or institutions.
4. Don't share fake news or unverified information — always 
   check before sharing.
5. Don't use social media as a platform to vent frustration 
   about college or professors.

---

## Key Reminder
Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can 
affect your internships, placements, and professional reputation 
tomorrow. Always think twice before you post. 🌐
